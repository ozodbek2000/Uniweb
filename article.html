Electronics suppliers warn of a shortage of premium headphones

Due to the refusal of premium headphone manufacturers (Sennheiser, Marshall, Sony) to supply their products to Russia, there may be a shortage of such products in the country.
According to the head of the "Accessories" direction
Due to the refusal of premium headphone manufacturers (Sennheiser, Marshall, Sony) to supply their products to Russia, there may be a shortage of such products in the country. Electronics distributors told Kommersant about this.

According to Olga Polonovskaya, Head of Accessories at diHouse, stocks of premium headphones will last for about one quarter.

Kommersant was unable to find such a product for sale on the websites of the largest retailers. The only headphones more expensive than 10 thousand rubles on the Citilink website are the Sennheiser HD 300 PRO (17.99 thousand rubles), which remained in stock in only one store. In M.Video, too, only one store has Beats Solo Pro (19.9 thousand rubles), and in Eldorado there are several models from Sony.

Russian marketplaces do not expect a shortage, relying on parallel imports. However, products supplied under this scheme often turn out to be counterfeit, experts warn.
Lee Campbell/Unsplash
According to the head of the "Accessories" direction
Russian marketplaces do not expect a shortage, relying on parallel imports. However, products supplied under this scheme often turn out to be counterfeit, experts warn.

After the start of the military operation in Ukraine, manufacturers of headphones and audio equipment JBL, Bose and Harman Kardon closed their Russian representative offices. Other vendors such as Marshall, Sennheiser, Samsung and Sony have not made any official announcements but are not currently shipping merchandise, Marvel Distribution says.
